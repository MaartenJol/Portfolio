About me

Hi, I'm Maarten Jol — a 26-year-old data enthusiast with a passion for football, analytics, and uncovering patterns in complex information. Originally from The Hague and now based in Rotterdam, I’ve always been drawn to how things work — especially in sports.

My interest in data began with a curiosity about stats behind the game: who scored the most, which teams dominated, and whether the numbers matched the stories. This led me from a Bachelor in Economics to a Master in Data Science & Marketing Analytics, where I honed my skills in statistics, programming, and data-driven storytelling. Since April 2024, I’ve been part of the Veneficus Talent Academy — a data traineeship that began with six weeks of intensive training in Python, SQL, machine learning, and cloud tools. I’m currently on assignment as a Data Specialist at PGGM, where I work with SQL and Power BI to identify data gaps and support clean-up strategies.

Outside of work, I build my own football databases, analyse match data, and apply data-driven strategies in fantasy football — all fuelled by a love for both numbers and sports. I’m meticulous, analytical, structured, and detail-oriented, with a drive to turn raw data into clear, impactful insights.

Projects

Masters' Thesis

Using 1,600+ Eredivisie matches from 2017 to 2023, this thesis examined how COVID-19 attendance restrictions impacted home advantage and referee decision-making. Multilevel modelling and six regression analyses revealed that home teams performed worse and referee bias declined in matches when fans were absent. The project highlights how crowd presence influences both performance and officiating, offering insights into behavioural patterns in football. The research strengthened my skills in statistical modelling, data wrangling, and presenting complex findings in a clear, evidence-based narrative. The R script used for the analysis is included in this portfolio.

Personal Football Stats Database(s)

Over the years, I’ve built a personal football database in Excel, tracking stats from major competitions like the Champions League, World Cup, European Championship, Eredivisie, Premier League, and my favourite teams Feyenoord and ADO Den Haag. I manually collect data from official sources (e.g. premierleague.com, Eredivisie.nl, Flashscore) and enjoy full control over the structure — deciding what to include and how to organise it. I regularly update these sheets, both for fun and to create football quizzes for friends and teammates. While not professionally built, this project reflects my passion for football data, attention to detail, and ability to work with structured information — skills I also apply in more technical environments using Python, SQL, and visualisation tools. An example from the history of the World Cup is included in this portfolio.

Scorito (Fantasy) Football

I’ve been playing Scorito for over ten years — a Dutch fantasy football game where players build squads within a budget and earn points based on real-life performances. I currently compete in both the Eredivisie and Premier League editions, which attract tens of thousands of players. To support my decision-making process, I’ve built Excel sheets to track player performance, combining in-game stats with real-world data like goals, assists, cards, and xG (from sources such as fbref.com). This blend of data and football intuition has proven successful: I finished 109th out of 47,000+ in the 2023–2024 Eredivisie season and am currently ranked 3rd out of nearly 10,000 in the Premier League game. This project — documented in my portfolio with an example Excel file from the 2023–2024 Eredivisie season — reflects my passion for football data, strategic thinking, and structured analysis.
