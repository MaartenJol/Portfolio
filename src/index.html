Masters' Thesis

Using over 1,600 Eredivisie matches from 2017 to 2023, this thesis analysed how attendance restrictions during the COVID-19 pandemic influenced both home advantage and referee decision-making. By applying multilevel modelling and running six regression models (linear and Poisson), I found that home teams performed significantly worse — in terms of goals, points, and key game statistics — when fans were absent. Referee bias also decreased in matches without attendance, as away teams received fewer yellow cards and committed fewer fouls. The research demonstrates how crowd presence can affect both team performance and officiating, offering valuable insights into behavioural patterns in football. This project sharpened my skills in statistical modelling, data wrangling, and presenting complex findings in a clear, evidence-based narrative.

Personal Football Stats Database(s)

Over the years, I’ve built a personal football database in Excel, tracking statistics from major competitions like the Champions League, World Cup, European Championship, Eredivisie, Premier League, the Dutch national team, and my favourite clubs: Feyenoord and ADO Den Haag. I collect this data manually from official online sources such as PremierLeague.com, Eredivisie.nl, Flashscore.com, and others. I enjoy having full control over the structure and content of these datasets — deciding which stats to include, how to organise them, and continuously refining them over time. This is an ongoing project, and I regularly update the spreadsheets for fun, but also use them to create football quizzes for friends or teammates. While not professionally built, this project reflects my passion for football data, attention to detail, and the desire to work with structured information — all qualities I bring into more technical environments involving Python, SQL, or visualisation tools.

Scorito (Fantasy) Football

For over ten years, I’ve participated in Scorito, a Dutch fantasy football game where players create a squad under a budget and earn points based on real-life performances. I play both the Eredivisie and Premier League editions, which now attract tens of thousands of participants. Alongside the game, I’ve built Excel sheets to track player performance — combining official in-game stats with real-world data like goals, assists, and cards. In my analysis, I also consider advanced metrics such as expected goals (xG) from platforms like fbref.com to support decision-making. This blend of data and football intuition has proven successful for me: in the 2023–2024 Eredivisie season I finished 109th out of 47,000+ participants, and in the current Premier League season I’m ranked 3rd out of nearly 10,000.
